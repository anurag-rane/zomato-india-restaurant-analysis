# Key Findings & Business Insights

## Dataset
12,137 unique restaurant-location combinations across Bangalore (deduplicated from 56,251 rows)

## Ratings Overview
- Rating distribution follows a bell curve peaking at 3.7–3.8
- Average rating across all restaurants: ~3.7 out of 5
- Only 55 restaurants achieve a 4.9 rating — top 0.4% of the market
- Most restaurants (60%+) cluster between 3.5 and 4.0

## Top Locations
- Lavelle Road leads with 4.07 average rating across 52 restaurants
- Sankey Road second at 4.04 — high quality, low competition (only 12 restaurants)
- Koramangala appears 3 times in top 10 — 3rd Block, 4th Block, 5th Block
- Rajarajeshwari Nagar leads among high-volume locations in the cleaned dataset

## Cuisine Analysis
- North Indian dominates by volume (2,197 restaurants) but rates lower (3.53)
- Continental has far fewer restaurants (287) but rates highest at 3.87
- Biryani has 548 restaurants but lowest avg rating at 3.48 — most competitive
- Desserts and Cafes rate well (3.81, 3.77) — strong niche opportunity
- Fast Food volume is high but rating is below average — oversaturated segment

## Cost vs Rating
- Fine Dining (₹1000+) rates highest at 3.97 — low competition, high expectation
- Mid-range (₹300–600) is the volume sweet spot — most restaurants, most customers
- Budget (<₹300) and Mid-range both average 3.54 — price alone doesn't drive ratings
- Premium (₹600–1000) hits the best balance of rating and market size

## Online Ordering Impact
- Restaurants with online ordering average 3.65 vs 3.59 without
- 737 restaurants offer online ordering vs 198 that don't — majority enabled
- Positive correlation between online ordering and ratings — likely driven by review volume

## Underserved Opportunities
- Sankey Road: 4.04 avg rating, only 12 restaurants — high demand, low supply
- Several smaller neighbourhoods show avg ratings ≥4.0 with strong vote counts
- These signal unmet demand worth targeting for new restaurant operators

## Actionable Recommendations
1. New operators should target Sankey Road and similar high-rating low-density areas
2. Continental and specialty cuisines offer better rating outcomes with less competition
3. Enable online ordering from day one — consistent positive correlation with ratings
4. Fine dining in premium neighbourhoods has the highest rating ceiling
5. Avoid entering Biryani and Fast Food segments without strong differentiation
6. Mid-range pricing (₹300–600) maximises customer reach with acceptable margins
